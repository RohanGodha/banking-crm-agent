# RM–Client FAQs — Procedural Knowledge Base (Referenced in Real Time)

> Authoritative FAQ document for the RM Copilot. The assistant retrieves answers from
> this file in real time and grounds its reply, rather than relying on the LLM's memory
> or only the structured database.

## A. Applying for a loan

1. **How does a client apply for a loan?**
   Share income and KYC documents, the RM checks eligibility (CIBIL, FOIR, salary trend),
   submits the application, the bank sanctions, the client e-signs the agreement, and funds
   are disbursed to the client's account.

2. **What documents are needed for a loan?**
   Identity (Aadhaar/PAN/Passport), address proof, last 3 months' salary slips, 6 months'
   bank statements, latest Form 16 / ITR, and a photograph. Secured loans add asset papers.

3. **How long does approval take?**
   Pre-approved/salary customers: minutes to a few hours. Fresh personal loans: 1–3 days.
   Home loans (with property/legal checks): 5–15 working days.

4. **What decides eligibility?**
   CIBIL score, net monthly income, existing EMIs (FOIR ≤ 50%), employment stability, and
   age within the product's range.

5. **What is FOIR / DTI?**
   Fixed Obligations to Income Ratio = total EMIs ÷ net monthly income. Keep it ≤ 50% to
   improve approval odds and ticket size.

6. **Can a co-applicant help?**
   Yes — clubbing a spouse/parent's income improves eligibility and can lower the rate.

7. **What is a pre-approved offer?**
   A pre-qualified limit based on the client's salary and on-us behaviour; minimal documents
   and instant disbursal.

## B. The RM's role

8. **How does an RM help the client?**
   Single point of contact who understands the profile, recommends suitable products,
   prepares the file, negotiates pricing within policy, and shepherds the application to
   disbursal — then services it afterwards.

9. **Will the RM get the best rate?**
   The RM positions the client in the right risk band and applies eligible relationship
   pricing; final rate follows the policy grid for that profile.

10. **What happens after disbursal?**
    The RM tracks repayment health, flags reset/top-up opportunities, and assists with
    statements, foreclosure, and grievances.

11. **How does the RM prioritise outreach?**
    By a blend of customer value and conversion propensity, with urgency for churn-risk and
    retention signals — the highest-value, most-likely, most-urgent clients first.

## C. KYC process

12. **What is KYC?**
    Know Your Customer — verifying identity and address with Officially Valid Documents
    before opening an account or extending credit.

13. **What are valid KYC documents (OVDs)?**
    Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving Licence (plus a recent photograph).

14. **What is CKYC?**
    The Central KYC Registry — once KYC is done, a 14-digit CKYC number lets the client reuse
    it across regulated entities without redoing it.

15. **What is video KYC (V-CIP)?**
    RBI-permitted remote KYC over a live video call with an official — used for fully digital
    onboarding.

16. **How often is KYC re-verified?**
    Periodically by risk: high-risk every 2 years, medium 8 years, low 10 years.

17. **Is PAN mandatory?**
    Yes for credit facilities above thresholds, and PAN must be linked to Aadhaar.

## D. Repayment, rates and changes

18. **How is EMI calculated?**
    EMI = P·r·(1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1), where P = principal, r = monthly rate, n = months.

19. **Fixed vs floating rate?**
    Fixed stays constant for tenure; floating (EBLR) resets with the RBI repo rate at least
    every 3 months.

20. **Are there prepayment/foreclosure charges?**
    No foreclosure or prepayment penalty on floating-rate loans to individuals. Fixed-rate
    loans may carry a charge per the agreement.

21. **What is a loan top-up?**
    Additional funds over an existing good-standing loan (common on home loans) at attractive
    rates, with minimal fresh documentation.

22. **What is a balance transfer?**
    Moving an existing loan to another lender for a lower rate; the RM can counter with a
    retention rate or top-up to keep the client.

23. **What if the client misses an EMI?**
    A flat penal charge applies (not compounding penal interest), and the DPD is reported to
    bureaus. The RM should proactively offer restructuring for genuine stress.

## E. Records, rights and protection

24. **How does the client check their CIBIL score?**
    One free full report per bureau per year (CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, CRIF); 750+ is strong.

25. **What is the Key Facts Statement (KFS)?**
    A mandatory one-page disclosure with the APR, EMI, all fees, and total cost of credit —
    the client should read it before signing.

26. **Is the client's deposit protected?**
    Yes — DICGC insures deposits up to ₹5,00,000 per depositor per bank.

27. **What are the client's rights on recovery?**
    Recovery agents may contact only between 8:00 and 19:00, must identify themselves, and
    cannot harass; grievances go to the bank's nodal officer and then the RBI Ombudsman.

28. **How is the client's data handled?**
    KYC and credit data are used only for the facility, shared with bureaus as required by
    regulation, and protected under the bank's privacy policy.

## F. Quick relationship tips for the RM

29. **Best time to pitch a loan?**
    On a salary hike, a repo-rate cut (cheaper EMIs), near a loan's closure, or after a large
    discretionary spend that signals an upcoming financing need.

30. **How to build trust?**
    Lead with the client's benefit, disclose the all-in cost up front via the KFS, and never
    over-promise a rate the policy grid can't support.
