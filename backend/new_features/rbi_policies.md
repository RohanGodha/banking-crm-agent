# RBI Policies — Lending, Banking Law & Interest Rates (Reference Knowledge Base)

> Indicative reference data for the RM Copilot knowledge base. Rates and limits are
> representative of Indian retail banking norms and should be confirmed against the
> latest RBI circulars before any customer commitment.

## 1. RBI policy rate instruments

| Instrument | What it is | Indicative value |
|---|---|---|
| Repo rate | Rate at which RBI lends to banks against securities | 6.50% |
| Reverse repo rate | Rate at which RBI borrows from banks | 3.35% |
| Standing Deposit Facility (SDF) | Floor of the liquidity corridor | 6.25% |
| Marginal Standing Facility (MSF) | Emergency overnight borrowing rate | 6.75% |
| Bank rate | Long-term lending rate to banks | 6.75% |
| Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR) | Share of deposits held with RBI in cash | 4.50% |
| Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR) | Share of deposits in liquid assets (G-Secs) | 18.00% |

Why it matters to an RM: when the repo rate moves, floating-rate (EBLR-linked) loan
EMIs reset, which is a natural trigger for refinance and retention conversations.

## 2. How retail loan interest rates are set

- **EBLR (External Benchmark Lending Rate):** since Oct 2019, most retail floating loans
  (home, personal, MSME) are linked to an external benchmark — usually the RBI repo rate.
  Final rate = repo rate + spread (credit risk premium + operating cost).
- **MCLR (Marginal Cost of Funds based Lending Rate):** older internal benchmark, still used
  for some legacy and corporate loans.
- **Fixed vs floating:** fixed locks the rate for tenure; floating resets with the benchmark.
- **Reset frequency:** EBLR loans must be reset at least once every 3 months.

## 3. Loan regulations and borrower protections

1. **Fair Practices Code (FPC):** banks must disclose all-in cost, fees, and the annualised
   rate before sanction; no hidden charges.
2. **Key Facts Statement (KFS):** mandatory standardised one-pager with APR, EMI, total
   cost of credit, and recovery contact for retail and MSME loans.
3. **No foreclosure / prepayment penalty on floating-rate loans** taken by individuals.
4. **Reset transparency:** on a benchmark reset, banks must offer the option to switch to
   fixed, and disclose the impact on EMI or tenure.
5. **Penal charges, not penal interest:** since Apr 2024, defaults attract a flat penal
   *charge*, not a compounding penal *interest rate*.
6. **Recovery agent conduct:** contact only between 8:00 and 19:00; no harassment; identity
   disclosure mandatory.
7. **Moratorium / restructuring:** permitted under RBI resolution frameworks for genuine
   stress, with credit-bureau reporting of "restructured" status.
8. **CKYC:** Central KYC Registry — KYC done once is reusable across regulated entities.
9. **Cooling-off / look-up period:** digital lending borrowers may exit during the cooling
   period by repaying principal + proportionate APR without penalty.
10. **Digital Lending Guidelines (2022):** all disbursals and repayments flow directly
    between the borrower's and the regulated entity's accounts — no pass-through pooling.

## 4. Priority Sector Lending (PSL)

- Banks must lend **40% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit** to priority sectors.
- Sub-targets: **18% agriculture**, **7.5% micro-enterprises**, **12% weaker sections**.
- Relevant when positioning agri, MSME, and small-ticket housing loans.

## 5. KYC and account norms (regulatory)

- **KYC documents:** Officially Valid Documents (OVD) — Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID,
  Driving Licence.
- **PAN mandatory** for credit facilities above prescribed thresholds; PAN–Aadhaar linkage
  required.
- **Periodic KYC update:** high-risk every 2 years, medium 8 years, low 10 years.
- **Loan exposure reporting** to credit bureaus (CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, CRIF) is
  mandatory and refreshed monthly.

## 6. Twenty quick factual data points (for fast RM recall)

1. CIBIL score range: **300–900**; 750+ is considered strong.
2. A score of **NA/NH** means no credit history (new-to-credit).
3. Credit bureaus in India: **CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, CRIF High Mark**.
4. Home loan **LTV**: up to **90%** for loans ≤ ₹30L, **80%** for ₹30L–₹75L, **75%** above ₹75L.
5. Recommended **FOIR/DTI** (EMIs to income): keep **≤ 50%**.
6. Personal loan tenure: typically **12–60 months**.
7. Home loan tenure: up to **30 years**.
8. Free credit report: **one full report per bureau per year** by regulation.
9. Hard enquiry impact: multiple loan enquiries in a short window can lower the score.
10. **DPD** = Days Past Due; 0 DPD is a clean record.
11. **EMI formula:** EMI = P·r·(1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1), where r = monthly rate, n = months.
12. GST of **18%** applies on loan processing fees and most banking charges.
13. **Section 80C:** home loan principal deduction up to **₹1.5L/year**.
14. **Section 24(b):** home loan interest deduction up to **₹2L/year** (self-occupied).
15. Credit utilisation ratio below **30%** supports a healthy score.
16. **Repo-linked loans** reset within **3 months** of a rate change.
17. Floating-rate retail loans have **zero** foreclosure charges for individuals.
18. **Deposit insurance (DICGC):** up to **₹5,00,000** per depositor per bank.
19. Minimum credit card payment is usually **5%** of the outstanding.
20. **Co-applicant** can improve eligibility by clubbing incomes and improving FOIR.
